Individual Therapy

Therapy for Men

You don't have to keep carrying this alone.

Most men who come to therapy have been dealing with their struggles for a long time before they pick up the phone. Maybe you've been told — by family, by friends, by the culture around you — that men handle things on their own. That asking for help is weakness.

Here's the truth: the strongest thing you can do is face what's really going on. And that's exactly what individual therapy is about.

Common issues men bring to therapy:

  • Anxiety that won't quiet down — constant worry, restlessness, difficulty being present
  • Depression or a persistent sense of emptiness, numbness, or "going through the motions"
  • Anger that feels out of proportion — snapping at your partner, kids, or coworkers
  • Feeling lost after a major life change — divorce, job loss, becoming a father, midlife transition
  • Difficulty opening up or connecting emotionally with the people who matter most
  • Struggling with identity — who am I beyond my job title, my role as provider, my achievements?
  • Relationship patterns that keep repeating no matter who you're with

What to expect

Sessions are 50 minutes, typically weekly. In our work together, we'll move beyond surface-level coping strategies and get to the root of what's driving your pain. I use a relational, attachment-informed approach — which means we'll look at how your earliest relationships shaped the way you connect (or don't connect) with others today.

This isn't about lying on a couch and recounting your childhood. It's active, honest, sometimes uncomfortable work — and it leads to real, lasting change.
